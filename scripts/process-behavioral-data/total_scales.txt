Self-Report Assessments Relevant to Disordered Emotional States

    Achenbach Adult Self-Report (ASR)
        Measures a wide range of psychological problems, including anxiety, depression, and social withdrawal.

    Beck Depression Inventory (BDI)
        Assesses the severity of depressive symptoms.

    Positive and Negative Affect Schedule (PANAS)
        Measures positive and negative affect, providing an assessment of emotional states.

    State-Trait Anxiety Inventory (STAI)
        Differentiates between state anxiety (temporary condition) and trait anxiety (general tendency).

    Perceived Stress Scale (PSS)
        Evaluates the degree to which situations in one's life are appraised as stressful.

    Center for Epidemiologic Studies Depression Scale (CES-D)
        Screens for symptoms of depression in the general population.

    Life Satisfaction Questionnaire (LSQ)
        Assesses overall life satisfaction and well-being.

    Big Five Inventory (BFI)
        Measures five major dimensions of personality, including neuroticism, which is closely related to emotional instability.

    Connor-Davidson Resilience Scale (CD-RISC)
        Assesses resilience, the ability to cope with stress and adversity.

    Emotional Regulation Questionnaire (ERQ)
        Evaluates individual differences in the habitual use of two emotion regulation strategies: cognitive reappraisal and expressive suppression.

    Multidimensional Personality Questionnaire (MPQ)
        Measures a range of personality traits, including negative emotionality.

    Social Interaction Anxiety Scale (SIAS)
        Assesses anxiety related to social interactions.

    Fear of Negative Evaluation Scale (FNE)
        Measures apprehension about being negatively evaluated by others.

    Brief Symptom Inventory (BSI)

    Penn Emotion Recognition Test (ER40)
        Assesses the ability to recognize facial expressions of emotion.

    Adult Self-Report (ASR)
        Measures a broad range of emotional and behavioral problems.
